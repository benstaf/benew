# How YC Bench Works

YC Bench is a live benchmark platform designed to do one thing: predict which Y Combinator companies will become the next generation of breakout startups — before the rest of the world figures it out.

## The Problem We're Solving

Every YC batch contains hundreds of companies. Some will grow into billion-dollar businesses. Most won't. The challenge isn't identifying *that* difference in hindsight — it's identifying it early, while it still matters for investors, operators, and the companies themselves.

Traditional due diligence lags. Media coverage lags even more. YC Bench runs in real time.

## The Velocity Signal

Our core insight is simple: **early-stage momentum is predictive**. Not vanity metrics — not follower counts or press mentions — but *cohort-relative velocity*.

We track a company's trajectory across several dimensions:

- **Hiring velocity** — pace of team growth relative to cohort peers
- **Product signal** — public-facing indicators of shipping cadence
- **Community traction** — organic growth in relevant communities
- **Founder activity** — speaking, publishing, and industry presence

Each signal is normalized against the cohort baseline, so a company that's growing at 2× the cohort average scores higher than one growing at absolute terms but slower than its peers.

## Live Scoring, Not Snapshots

Most benchmarks are quarterly snapshots. YC Bench updates continuously. When a company crosses a momentum threshold, its score adjusts within hours — not months.

This creates a live leaderboard that reflects reality as it unfolds, not as analysts interpreted it three months ago.

## What the Scores Mean

Scores are indexed to 100 at cohort launch. A company at 180 on day 90 has grown at 1.8× baseline velocity since Demo Day. Our backtesting across W21 and S22 cohorts shows that companies above 150 on day 90 had a **4.2× higher probability** of closing a Series A within 18 months compared to those below cohort average.

## What We Don't Do

We don't use insider information. We don't track revenue (private companies don't disclose it). We don't factor in founder backgrounds, school pedigree, or subjective assessments. The benchmark is signal-driven, not narrative-driven.

## Where We're Going

The next version of YC Bench will incorporate model predictions — letting users submit their own rankings at Demo Day and scoring them against outcomes six and twelve months later. The best predictors will be surfaced and recognized.
